Understanding the Grades
  • Grade A (Stars): Rare, high-demand IPOs (e.g. over 50x oversubscribed). High Day 1 pop but hard to get allocated.
  • Grade B (Workhorses): Consistent, standard IPOs. Slightly smaller average pops but high frequency. Excellent for compounding.
  • Grade C (Landmines): High-risk IPOs that fail to perform. Total returns are -237.7%—we skip these completely.

What is 5% Average Allocation?

Because of high demand, retail applications are oversubscribed. If you apply for RM 10,000, you will usually only get allocated about 5% (RM 500) worth of shares. The other RM 9,500 cash is immediately refunded to your bank account.

What do the Columns mean?
  • Fixed: You apply for RM 10k every time, but you withdraw your profits instead of reinvesting. You grow slowly.
  • Compounded (Cash): You add your profits back to your capital pool to apply for larger share volumes on subsequent IPOs.
  • Hunter Pro (2x Leverage): You compound cash profits and use preferred broker financing to double your application power, multiplying your returns.

Leverage is a Speed Multiplier: By using 2x leverage financing, you double your share allocation per IPO. This accelerates compounding exponentially. In 7 years, the difference between Cash and Leverage is a staggering RM 1.9 Million.

Core Strategy Rules

Recycle Capital

Capital is returned after listing. You only need the "Apply per IPO" amount in your account.

Skip Grade C

Historical data shows Grade C destroys capital (-237%). Never apply for C-rated IPOs.
