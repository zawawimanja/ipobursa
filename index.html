The Hunter Strategy Guide

A unified look at our grading system and execution rules.

Grade A

SWING

Main Market / GLC giants. High institutional support. The Goal: Hold for institutional accumulation throughout Day 1.

  • OS: > 20x
  • PE: Stable (< 15x)
Pro Tip: Look for Tech/AI sectors. If OS is high, hold until 5:00 PM close for maximum gain.

Grade B

SCALP

ACE Market growth plays. High retail hype. The Goal: Capture the initial morning spike and exit within 30 mins.

  • OS: > 50x (Required)
  • Sector: Data Centers / Solar
Rule: Sell between 9:00 AM – 9:05 AM. Don't be greedy; the hype often fades by lunch.

Grade C

AVOID

Low interest or high valuation. Warning: High risk of "Full Allocation" where you get 100% of requested shares.

  • OS: < 10x
  • PE: Overpriced (> 20x)
Danger: If you get everything you applied for, sell immediately at the open.

Main Market vs ACE Market

Which one should you choose? Here is the simple difference:

MM

Main Market

  • Established, big companies
  • High profit track record required
  • Generally considered "safer" for beginners
ACE

ACE Market

  • Fast-growing, smaller companies
  • High growth potential
  • Higher risk, higher volatility

Beginner Tip: Start with Main Market (MM) IPOs first as they are usually more stable!

The 5-Step IPO Journey

New to IPOs? Here is how a company goes public in Malaysia:

1. Draft Stage

The company shares its "plan" (Prospectus) with the authorities. No buying yet—this is for checking and feedback.

2. MITI Stage

A "special invite" phase. Before everyone else, a portion is offered to Bumiputera investors through MITI.

3. Public Stage

Go time! Normal investors can apply for shares through their online bank (e-IPO).

4. Pre-Listing Stage

Balloting results are announced. The shares are credited to your CDS account if you win.

5. Listed Stage

The finish line. The company starts trading on Bursa Malaysia, and the price starts moving!

The "Cheat Sheet" to IPO Data

Confused by all the numbers? Here is what you should focus on as a beginner:

Fair Value (FV)

The "Expert's Guess." Analysts calculate what the share is *actually* worth. If the FV is higher than the IPO price, it's considered "undervalued" (a good deal!).

P/E Ratio

The "Price Tag." It tells you how much you are paying for every RM1 of profit. Usually, you want this to be lower than the sector average.

Oversubscription

The "Popularity Test." If it's 10x, it means 10 people want every 1 share. Higher usually means people are very confident.

Market Cap

The "Size." Total value of the company. Big ones (Sunway) are safer; small ones (OGX) grow faster but are riskier.

Hunter's Checklist

Before you can buy any IPO, you MUST have these two things ready:

1. CDS Account

An account to hold your shares. You can open one via the Bursa Anywhere app or your broker.

2. e-Banking Account

Most Malaysian banks (Maybank, CIMB, Public Bank, etc.) allow you to apply for IPOs via their "e-IPO" menu.
